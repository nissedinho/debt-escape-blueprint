How the trap works

They Designed It This Way on Purpose

In the 1970s, banks discovered that customers who paid minimums were far more profitable than those who paid in full. So they lobbied to keep minimum payments low — just 1-2% of your balance — which means most of your payment goes to interest, not principal.

The result: a $5,000 balance at 22% APR takes over 10 years to pay off on minimums. You'll pay $4,200 in interest on a $5,000 purchase. The bank doesn't want you to know this. They definitely don't want you to call and ask for a rate cut.
